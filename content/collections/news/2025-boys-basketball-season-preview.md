The Chiefs boys basketball program enters the 2025-26 season with arguably its most experienced roster in years, and head coach Marcus Thompson isn't hiding his ambitions. "We have the pieces to do something special this year," Thompson said at the team's media day. "The question is whether we execute when it matters."

**Veteran Leadership**

Returning senior guard Jordan Hayes headlines a squad that brings back four of five starters from last year's 14-8 campaign. Hayes, who averaged 18.2 points per game as a junior, has spent the offseason working on his mid-range game and arrives at camp in the best shape of his career. Alongside him is junior Marcus Bell, a versatile forward who drew interest from several Division II programs at last summer's showcase events. Point guard Darius Washington rounds out the senior trio, providing veteran leadership and a steady hand running Thompson's up-tempo offense.

**A Young Core Developing Fast**

The supporting cast is the most encouraging part of the picture. Sophomore center Calvin Brooks emerged late last season as a legitimate interior presence, and sophomore swingman Eli Patterson showed flashes of being a reliable secondary scorer. Freshman Isaiah Turner has drawn rave reviews in practice for his shooting touch and basketball IQ. "We've never had this kind of depth going into November," Thompson said.

**The Schedule**

The Chiefs open with a home game against Republic on November 18 before hitting the road the following week at Ozark. Conference play begins in earnest after the holiday break. The team faces a balanced schedule with each of the area's top programs appearing at least twice. A strong showing at the Kickapoo Holiday Tournament in late December could provide valuable experience — and momentum — heading into the stretch run.
