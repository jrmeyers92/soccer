Spring sports season is here, and the Chiefs athletic program has plenty to be excited about. Baseball, softball, and track and field all open their seasons this week, and early signs point to competitive campaigns across the board.

**Baseball: Rotation Depth a Strength**

Head coach Roberto Garcia enters his fifth year with what he calls "the most talented pitching staff I've had here." Senior ace Ryan Kowalski is the centerpiece — a hard-throwing right-hander who went 8-2 last spring and returns as one of the area's most coveted prospects. Behind him, junior Josh Lee and senior Grant Tillman give the Chiefs three legitimate starters. Offensively, shortstop Carlos Rivera (.352 average last spring) and first baseman Jordan Webb (7 home runs in 2024) form one of the most productive middle-of-the-order combos in the conference. The varsity squad opens March 25 at home against Republic.

**Softball: Torres Headlines Loaded Roster**

The Lady Chiefs softball program is coming off back-to-back winning seasons, and with senior pitcher Megan Torres returning, another strong showing is expected. Torres struck out 94 batters last spring with an ERA under 2.00, and her presence in the circle gives the Chiefs a genuine ace. Junior outfielder Keisha Thomas provides the offensive spark, while a deep lineup means opponents can't key on any single hitter. "We have a group that trusts each other," said Coach Garcia. "That makes a big difference in a sport where execution is everything."

**Track & Field: Depth Across All Events**

Coach Sarah Chen's track and field program has grown significantly in participation over the past three years, and the 2025 squad reflects that. On the boys side, senior sprinter Darius Coleman is a legitimate state-qualifying candidate in the 100m. Junior distance runner Marcus Whitley anchors the middle-distance events. The girls program is led by junior Amara Diallo, who set a program record in the 1600m during an indoor meet in February. Both teams feature depth in the relay events that could prove decisive in close dual meets. The first outdoor meet is March 22 at home.
