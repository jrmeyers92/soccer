The Kickapoo Chiefs girls soccer program kicked off the spring 2026 season in style Tuesday night, with both the varsity and JV squads picking up opening wins at home against Republic High School.

**Varsity dominates 3-0**

The varsity squad looked sharp from the opening whistle. Junior forward Natalie Thompson bagged a brace in the first 30 minutes, and Addison King added a third after halftime to put the result beyond any doubt. Goalkeeper Victoria Wright earned a clean sheet in a commanding performance that has fans excited about what this group can do.

"We've been preparing all offseason for this," said head coach Coach Stevens. "The girls came out with great energy and executed the game plan really well."

**JV wins 2-0**

The JV team followed up with their own clean-sheet victory. Freshman forward Emma Carter and sophomore Luna Wilson each scored as the young Chiefs showed plenty of promise in their season debut.

The Chiefs are back in action on February 24th, with both squads traveling to Nixa.
