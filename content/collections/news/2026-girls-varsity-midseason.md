Midway through the spring schedule, the Kickapoo Chiefs girls varsity soccer team is one of the hottest programs in the area. Through six games the Chiefs have put together a 5-0-1 record, outscoring opponents 15-2 and showing the kind of consistent quality that has the team eyeing an unbeaten regular season.

**Thompson leading the way**

Junior forward Natalie Thompson is in the form of her life. With 8 goals and 3 assists through six games, she's become one of the most dangerous attackers in the conference. She recorded a hat trick at Willard (4-0) and has scored in four consecutive matches.

"Natalie's been excellent," said Coach Stevens. "But what's impressed me most is how the whole group has raised their level."

**Defense holding firm**

The back line, anchored by seniors Hailey Scott, Paisley Nelson, and Savannah Rivera, has been remarkable. Victoria Wright has three clean sheets and has made some crucial stops to preserve results.

The lone blemish on the record is a 1-1 draw at home against Ozark in early March — the only game where the Chiefs didn't take all three points.

**What's next**

The Chiefs host Bolivar on April 1st before a stretch of away fixtures in April. If they can stay healthy and maintain their current form, this squad has the talent to go undefeated.
