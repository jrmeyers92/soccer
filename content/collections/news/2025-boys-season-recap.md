With the dust settled on the 2025 fall season, it's time to look back at what was a strong year for both the varsity and JV boys soccer programs.

**Varsity: 7-2-1**

The varsity squad finished with a record of 7 wins, 2 losses, and 1 draw — one of the program's better regular-season records in recent memory. Junior forward Mason Rodriguez led the offense with 8 goals and 5 assists, earning recognition as one of the top strikers in the area. Midfielders Connor White and Kyle Young provided the engine in the middle, combining for 15 goal contributions on the season.

The low point was the early September loss at Ozark (1-3), but the team responded with four consecutive wins — a stretch that defined the season. Senior goalkeeper Evan Clark recorded 4 shutouts and was a steady presence in goal all year.

"This group showed real character," said head coach Jeff Rogers. "We had some adversity early but figured it out. Really proud of how these guys competed."

**JV: 6-2-2**

The JV team also had a solid year, finishing 6-2-2. Sophomore Tyler Johnson led the way with 5 goals, and the squad showed encouraging depth across the lineup. Several freshmen on this roster look like future contributors at the varsity level.

We thank all the players, coaches, and families who made the 2025 season a success. Eyes now turn to spring as the girls programs take the field in 2026.
