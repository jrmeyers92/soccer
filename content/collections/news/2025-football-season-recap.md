The 2025 Chiefs football season will be remembered as a turning point for the program. A 7-3 regular season record and a District playoff berth was the reward for a squad that overcame an early-season hiccup to reel off six wins in their final eight games.

**Offense Finds its Footing**

Junior quarterback Drew Harmon was the story of the season. After a promising freshman year in limited action, Harmon settled in as one of the area's most dangerous signal-callers in 2025, throwing for over 2,100 yards and 22 touchdowns against just 5 interceptions. "Drew grew up this year," said head coach Steve Miller. "In August I wasn't sure what we had. By October I knew we had something special."

Senior running back Marcus Tate provided the ground game to keep defenses honest, rushing for over 1,100 yards and 14 touchdowns. The pair gave opposing defensive coordinators fits all fall. Wide receivers Jayden Price and Caleb Monroe combined for 13 touchdown receptions, while tight end Owen Blackwell emerged as a red-zone favorite.

**Defense Anchors the Stretch Run**

The second-half surge was fueled by a defense that found its identity. Defensive end Elijah Santos was a terror off the edge, recording 9 sacks and regularly affecting opposing quarterbacks even when not registering a stat. Senior linebacker Kofi Asante led the team in tackles and brought energy to every game.

The two losses in the second half — at Ozark and at home to Kickapoo — came against the area's two other playoff teams, making the defeats more understandable in hindsight. With a sophomore-heavy supporting cast around Harmon, expectations are already building for 2026.
